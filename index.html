X steps to building better mobile products

Step 1: Assume you have no idea what your customer wants

We can't solve problems that people don't realize they have. Let's work with our communities find out what their issues really are and find unique ways to address them. Just being the 'leading source of < blank >' isn't enough to make you standout in the crowd anymore.

Step 2: Don't waste too much time picking ideas, just go forth and test any that have merit

When we pick ideas, people to more polticking and less building. Who needs more politicians?

Step 3: Gathering data isn't the same as applying data to improve your products

Nothing is more wasteful than taking the time to get the answers and then failing to implement them because you don't agree with them. The right thing to do has nothing to do with you. Get over yourself.

Step 4: Avoid the allure of vanity metrics

It is all to easy to rally the workplace around total number of registered users. It's much harder to feel good about that number if few of them are coming back. However, if you have few registered users but a high return rate you may have something to build on and a true metric to celebrate.

Step 5: Accept that the web is the ultimate multi-platform strategy

Responsive, mobile-mindset first. Make the market declare a need for anything that doesn't require a native app at launch and test your market rigorously before making such an investment of time or resources.

Step 6: Use public data to build customer relationships or lose them forever

Data can allow you to interact with your audience at an intimate level and apply personal focus to the biggest stories and issues that impact their lives. If you can't find a way to take public data and enrich the lives of your audience, they won't be your audience for long.

Step 7: Release the hounds

The power of the press has been distributed to the masses. Not everyone can use this power wisely, but I am pretty sure the people you have bothered to employ can. Listen to them and give them room to explore for you. If you don't, you'll likely see them do it on their own... against you.

Step 8: More awesome please

There is an opportunity cost incurred every time we sit down and dedicate resources to rehash an app or website or cms that already exists. Focus your attention on building things that weren't even conceivable 5 years or 2 years ago. Doing so will give you the best shot to move the needle within your organization.
